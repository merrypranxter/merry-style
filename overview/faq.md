# Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### What is New Age Glam Fantasy?

A retro-mystical visual style combining late-1970s to mid-1980s metaphysical paperback art, glam fantasy cinema, soft-focus beauty photography, prismatic pastel-neon color, and VHS/CRT analog decay.

### Is this just "vaporwave" or "synthwave"?

No. While they share retro elements:
- **Vaporwave** is ironic, collage-based, consumer-culture focused
- **Synthwave** is hard-neon, automotive, grid-based, electronic
- **New Age Glam Fantasy** is sincere, mystical, glamorous, soft-focused, spiritually aspirational

### Can men be subjects in this style?

Yes, though the style has feminine-coded roots. Male subjects work best as:
- cosmic priests
- mystical guides
- androgynous glam figures
- symbolic archetypes rather than warrior types

### Is this style "camp"?

It can be, but it works best with sincere emotional investment. The style believes in its own vision of transcendence.

## Technical Questions

### What's the most important element?

The combination of **glamour + mysticism + analog wear**. Missing any one makes it drift toward generic fantasy, modern editorial, or clean surrealism.

### How much analog texture should I use?

Start subtle (Level 1-2):
- Light VHS softness
- Subtle chroma bleed
- Faint CRT feel
- Bloom-heavy highlights

Increase to Level 3 only for explicit "lost media" vibes.

### What if my images look too modern?

Add:
- VHS/CRT texture
- Faded print feel
- Era cues ("1978–1986")
- Metaphysical paperback framing
- Reduce sharpness, increase bloom

### What if my images look too generic?

Add:
- Specific motifs (crystals, mushrooms, mirrors)
- Tactile materials (satin, lamé, reflective water)
- Stronger era anchoring
- More symbolic structure

## Prompting Questions

### How long should my prompts be?

- **Short**: 50-80 words for quick tests
- **Medium**: 100-150 words for balanced control
- **Long**: 200+ words for hero images with high specificity

### Should I use negative prompts?

Yes, as guard rails. See [negative-prompts.md](../05_PROMPT_ENGINEERING/negative-prompts.md) for a starter list.

### What order should I put things in?

Recommended sequence:
1. Subject
2. Setting
3. Motifs
4. Styling
5. Lighting
6. Optics
7. Palette
8. Analog artifacts
9. Emotional tone
10. Composition
11. Style lock

### Can I use this for video?

Yes. Add motion cues:
- "slow camera drift"
- "haze swirling"
- "prism light dancing"
- "VHS tracking instability"

## Color Questions

### What colors work best?

**Core palette:**
- Hot pink / bubblegum pink
- Lavender / orchid / violet
- Teal / aqua / turquoise
- Lemon glow / peach
- Pearl white / silver

**Avoid heavy:**
- Primary red/blue/yellow
- Earth tones (brown, olive, rust)
- Black-heavy contrast

### Is rainbow okay?

Yes, if it feels:
- Prismatic (crystal refraction)
- Airbrushed
- Mist-softened
- Holographic

Not flat stripes or vector bands.

## Style Drift Questions

### My images keep going cyberpunk

Add:
- Mist, moonlight, ritual elegance
- Crystals, mirrors, temples
- Pastel-opal tones
- Remove: cities, signage, leather, hard neon

### My images look like generic AI ethereal

Add:
- Era anchors ("1978–1986")
- Tactile materials
- Specific motifs
- Analog artifacts
- Paperback/VHS framing

### My images look too dark/horror

Add:
- Pearl glow
- Pink/cyan/lavender aura
- Tenderness, devotional posture
- Remove: menace, blood, dread, demonic coding

## Workflow Questions

### Should I add analog effects in post?

Both approaches work:
- **Prompt directly**: Faster, more integrated
- **Add in post**: More control, can be stronger

Best workflow: Generate clean first, then layer analog wear.

### What tools work well for post-processing?

- Photoshop: bloom, chroma bleed, scanlines
- After Effects: VHS plugins, CRT effects
- DaVinci Resolve: color grading to pink/lavender/cyan
- Dedicated: Retrograde, VHS Cam, Glitché

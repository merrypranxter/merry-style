# Era Influences

## The golden period: 1978–1986

New Age Glam Fantasy is emotionally and visually anchored to the late 1970s through mid-1980s.

This was a unique period where:
- metaphysical paperback art flourished
- glam fantasy cinema peaked
- soft-focus beauty photography was dominant
- New Age culture went mainstream
- analog reproduction (VHS, print) was ubiquitous
- practical optical effects were the standard

## Why this era matters

### Pre-digital authenticity
Before CGI dominated, fantasy was built from:
- practical effects
- painted backdrops
- physical costumes
- optical compositing
- stage lighting

This gives the era a tactile, imperfect quality that digital often lacks.

### Metaphysical mainstreaming
The late 70s/early 80s was when:
- New Age went from fringe to mainstream
- crystals became household objects
- astrology columns were standard
- meditation entered popular culture
- "spiritual but not religious" became common

### Glamour persistence
While the 70s had glam rock and the 80s had excess, this period maintained:
- theatrical presentation
- beauty as performance
- costume as identity
- spectacle as communication

## What to avoid

Don't confuse the era with:
- 60s psychedelic poster art (too trippy, too graphic)
- 90s club culture (too electronic, too minimal)
- Y2K chrome futurism (too digital, too sleek)
- 70s folk bohemian (too earthy, too rustic)

The target is specifically **late 70s to mid 80s metaphysical glam**.

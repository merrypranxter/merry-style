# Analog Artifacts

## Purpose of this file

This document defines the analog damage, media wear, reproduction softness, and display-era texture that help make **New Age Glam Fantasy** feel like a recovered object rather than a clean contemporary render.

This layer is crucial. Without it, the style can still look pretty, but it often loses:
- temporal depth
- dream-memory feeling
- paperback / VHS / CRT credibility
- emotional nostalgia
- tactile media identity

---

## Core artifact statement

The analog artifact layer of New Age Glam Fantasy is **soft, worn, luminous, imperfect, and memory-bearing**.

It should feel like the image has been:
- printed
- copied
- screened
- taped
- aired
- photographed off a monitor
- transferred between formats
- faded in storage
- remembered through media damage

The goal is not aggressive glitch art. The goal is **romantic media age**.

---

## The seven artifact laws

### 1. Artifacts should soften, not destroy
Media wear should add atmosphere, tactility, and nostalgia. The damage should support beauty and dream logic.

### 2. VHS softness is more important than harsh glitch
Choose gentle analog blur and chroma drift over violent tearing, hard pixel breakup, or datamosh chaos.

### 3. Imperfection should feel era-specific
The artifact layer should suggest late 70s to mid-80s adjacent print / video / optical reproduction environments.

### 4. Artifacts should interact with color
Media wear often makes the palette more beautiful—magenta into cyan bleed, lavender haze drift, softened lemon aura clipping.

### 5. Light artifacts matter as much as texture artifacts
This style is strongly defined by what happens to highlights: halation, clipping, glow spread, edge bloom, overbright aura zones.

### 6. Analog age should remain readable but not always obvious
Sometimes the image should scream "lost VHS metaphysical fantasy." Sometimes it should only whisper "faded printed memory."

### 7. The image should still be desirable
Even damaged, it should feel glamorous, seductive, collectible, and beautiful. **Damaged treasure**, not ruined file.

## Main artifact families

### 1. VHS softness

**Description:** A soft, slightly smeared, analog-video blur that reduces crispness, blends edges, and gives a transferred, taped, or re-recorded feel.

**Visual traits:**
- lowered fine detail
- smeared highlights
- softened edges
- glow spread
- mild horizontal softness
- subtle image instability
- reduced sharp contrast
- emotional warmth through blur

**Prompt language:**
- VHS-soft image texture
- soft analog tape blur
- dreamlike VHS transfer softness
- generational tape softness
- faded VHS fantasy texture
- soft video-era blur

---

### 2. CRT scanline and screen texture

**Description:** A subtle screen-era display texture that gives the image the feeling of being seen through or captured from an older television or monitor.

**Visual traits:**
- faint horizontal scanlines
- soft phosphor-like glow
- light line structure in bright areas
- slight edge diffusion
- screen-emitted softness
- luminous monitor texture

**Prompt language:**
- faint CRT scanline texture
- soft cathode-ray glow
- vintage monitor softness
- screen-emission texture
- old television display feel
- luminous CRT surface grain

---

### 3. Chroma bleed and color drift

**Description:** Color edges, especially bright magenta, cyan, teal, pink, violet, and lemon highlights, spread or separate slightly in analog ways.

**Visual traits:**
- magenta-cyan edge bleed
- slight channel misalignment
- pink bloom into white
- teal fringes
- lavender spill
- soft color haloing
- analog color smearing

**Prompt language:**
- subtle chroma bleed
- analog color fringing
- magenta-cyan VHS bleed
- pastel channel drift
- soft RGB misregistration
- tape-era color spread

### 4. Bloom clipping and highlight halation

**Description:** Bright parts of the image bloom beyond their boundaries and sometimes approach soft clipping, especially around halos, jewelry, hair edges, crystals, and portals.

**Visual traits:**
- glowing highlight spill
- softened white cores
- halo enlargement
- bright edge fuzz
- dreamy overexposure

**Prompt language:**

- overbright aura edges
- soft clipping in sacred light zones
- glowing highlight bleed
- pearlescent halation

### 6. Film grain blended with analog video texture

**Description:** A very gentle grain layer mixed with video softness to keep the image tactile without making it too gritty.

**Visual traits:**

## Artifact intensity levels

### Level 1: Whispered age
**Best for:** portraits, elegant editorial variants, cleaner glamour scenes

**Traits:**
- slight bloom
- minor chroma softness
- faint CRT or print texture
- almost invisible VHS blur

**Reads as:** gently aged, retro-adjacent, subtly non-digital

---

### Level 2: Clear analog memory
**Best for:** most default New Age Glam Fantasy work, paperback covers, temple scenes, music-video stills

**Traits:**
- visible VHS softness
- moderate bloom
- light scanline feel
- color bleed
- mild print or tape wear

**Reads as:** found media, dream-memory object, recovered fantasy artifact

---

### Level 3: Heavy relic mode
**Best for:** explicit VHS references, occult TV special vibes, lost broadcast aesthetics, heavily archival pieces

**Traits:**
- obvious tape blur
- strong highlight bloom
- visible scan texture
- signal noise
- print/video wear layering

**Reads as:** damaged treasure, obscure tape archive, degraded visual relic

**Caution:** Even at heavy intensity, the image should remain attractive and legible.

---

## Default analog recommendation

If no specific artifact level is requested, a strong default is:
- light VHS softness
- subtle magenta-cyan chroma bleed
- bloom-heavy highlight halation
- faint CRT surface feel
- slight print-era softness
- gentle grain in darker areas
- minimal tracking instability

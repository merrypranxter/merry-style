# Aesthetic DNA

## Purpose of this file

This document breaks New Age Glam Fantasy into its deepest repeatable components.

If `core-definition.md` explains **what the style is**, this file explains **what the style is made of**.

Think of this as the internal anatomy of the aesthetic: the structural traits that keep it recognizable across portraits, landscapes, book covers, film stills, editorial frames, title cards, and moving-image prompts.

---

## Master definition

The DNA of New Age Glam Fantasy is the fusion of:
- **metaphysical symbolism**
- **glamour presentation**
- **soft optical radiance**
- **prismatic pastel-neon color**
- **analog memory texture**
- **cosmic emotional aspiration**

The style works when these traits are present **at the same time**, not as isolated details.

---

## The seven DNA strands

For practical use, the style can be divided into seven major strands:

1. Temporal DNA
2. Symbolic DNA
3. Glamour DNA
4. Optical DNA
5. Color DNA
6. Material DNA
7. Media DNA

An eighth strand is also useful in practice:
8. Emotional DNA

---

## 1. Temporal DNA

### Definition
The style belongs emotionally and visually to an imagined zone between the **late 1970s and mid-1980s**, especially the period where spiritual-commercial art, glam fantasy staging, paperback illustration, optical effects, and analog reproduction all overlap.

### Why it matters
Without era anchoring, the style drifts into generic celestial fantasy or modern glossy surrealism.

### Key temporal cues
- metaphysical paperback cover composition
- painted or airbrushed skies
- practical fog and stage light beams
- silver lamé, satin, chiffon, sequins, soft metallics
- VHS/CRT softness
- faded print palettes
- analog compositing sensibility
- pre-digital image logic
- heightened beauty styling associated with glam portraiture and fantasy cinema
- soft-focus visual romanticism rather than hyperreal digital sharpness

### Prompt language
- late-70s / early-80s metaphysical paperback aesthetic
- 1978–1986 glam sci-fantasy visual language
- lost VHS fantasy special
- vintage spiritual self-help cover art
- retro analog cosmic glamour
- faded paperback illustration finish
- practical optical effects era fantasy

### Common mistake
Using "retro" as a vague word without specifying what kind of retro.
The style is not 90s club, not 60s psychedelic poster art, not Y2K chrome futurism.

## 2. Symbolic DNA

### Definition
This style is never just decorative. It depends on symbols that imply destiny, revelation, initiation, divinity, healing, transformation, psychic power, or cosmic alignment.

### Why it matters
Symbolic structure is what separates New Age Glam Fantasy from ordinary fashion fantasy or pastel surrealism.

### Core symbolic categories

#### Celestial
- moons
- stars
- auroras
- planetary glow
- eclipses
- cosmic horizons
- halo suns

#### Initiatory
- portals
- staircases
- thresholds
- temple entrances
- gates
- mirrored passageways
- ascension platforms

#### Energetic
- crystals
- prisms
- aura fields
- refracted light
- beams
- glowing geometric forms

#### Devotional
- halos
- crowns
- ritual robes
- sacred poses
- offerings
- altar-like arrangements
- icon framing

#### Feminine-mythic
- priestess imagery
- oracle imagery
- cosmic queen archetypes
- swans, doves, white horses
- moon attendants
- divine beauty rituals

### Symbolic operating principle
Symbols should feel **legible but dreamy**.
They should suggest meaning without becoming dry diagram or hard occult instruction.

### Prompt language
- visionary threshold
- cosmic initiation
- sacred glamour ritual
- metaphysical transformation
- oracle chamber
- moonlit ascension
- luminous psychic temple
- mirrored portal of becoming

### Common mistake
Stuffing the frame with symbols that do not relate to one another.
A symbol system should feel like one mythology, not a random bag of mystical props.

## 3. Glamour DNA

### Definition
This style frames transcendence through glamour. Beauty is not optional decoration; it is part of the spiritual logic.

### Why it matters
Without glamour, the style becomes generic New Age illustration.
Without symbolism, it becomes generic fantasy beauty photography.
The fusion is the identity.

### Glamour components
- styled hair with volume, shape, movement, aura-like presence
- polished makeup with shimmer, gloss, frost, metallic accents
- satin, silk, lamé, chiffon, sequins, reflective fabrics
- sculptural jewelry
- manicured hands and elegant gesture language
- poised, ceremonial, iconic body language
- beauty-lighting emphasis on cheekbones, lips, eyes, skin glow

### Presentation rules
Glamour here should feel:
- ceremonial rather than casual
- iconic rather than documentary
- romantic rather than aggressive
- luminous rather than hard-edged
- theatrical rather than naturalistic

### Prompt language
- cosmic glamour portrait
- oracle beauty styling
- satin and silver ceremonial fashion
- soft-focus glam mystic
- prismatic beauty lighting
- celestial editorial glamour
- divine feminine icon framing

### Common mistake
Going too modern-fashion with sleek editorial minimalism.
This style wants heightened glamour, not restrained luxury branding.

---

## 4. Optical DNA

### Definition
The world of New Age Glam Fantasy is seen through softened, glowing, imperfect optics.
The image should feel filtered by atmosphere, diffusion, bloom, and dream memory.

### Why it matters
Optical behavior is one of the fastest ways to make the style believable.
Even strong motifs can fail if rendered with crisp, sterile, contemporary sharpness.

### Core optical traits
- halation around highlights
- soft-focus diffusion
- bloom around luminous edges
- haze and fog scatter
- pearlescent clipping
- slight lens softness
- gentle low-contrast glow
- prism flare
- chromatic fringe in bright transitions
- softened fine detail

### Optical mood
The image should feel:
- breathed on
- glowing through mist
- lightly overexposed in sacred areas
- softened by lens filtration
- suspended between physical image and memory image

### Prompt language
- heavy diffusion bloom
- dreamy halation
- glowing lens softness
- mist-filtered highlights
- pearlescent overexposure
- soft optical haze
- romantic analog diffusion
- prismatic lens flare crossing the frame

### Common mistake
Using "sharp cinematic" language.
Sharpness pushes the result toward modern digital fantasy unless deliberately balanced with haze and degradation.

## 5. Color DNA

### Definition
The color system is spectral, cosmetic, opalescent, and emotionally uplifted.
It lives between pastel softness and neon radiance.

### Why it matters
Color is one of the most recognizable parts of the style and one of the most easily mishandled.

### Core palette families

#### Prismatic pastels
- lavender
- blush pink
- icy cyan
- opal white
- powder blue
- peach glow
- pale mint
- pearl silver

#### Neon accents
- electric magenta
- hot pink highlights
- cyan beam edges
- ultraviolet-lilac flares
- aurora green
- gold-white glints

#### Divine neutrals
- cloud white
- moon silver
- champagne
- cream haze
- smoky mauve
- reflective black water
- midnight violet

### Color behavior
The palette should:
- glow rather than sit flat
- blend atmospherically
- move through gradients and iridescence
- support emotional softness
- allow strategic high-saturation accents
- feel cosmetic and celestial at once

### Prompt language
- opalescent pastel-neon palette
- aurora pink and cyan atmosphere
- lavender, pearl, and silver light
- rainbow prism refractions
- champagne glow and moonlit blue
- pastel spectral color drift

### Common mistake
Confusing this with synthwave.
Synthwave tends toward hard sunset gradients, black contrast, and graphic neon.
New Age Glam Fantasy is softer, more pearlescent, more spiritual, and more luminous.

## 6. Material DNA

### Definition
The style depends on tactile surfaces that catch light in mystical ways.

### Why it matters
Materials determine how the scene glows.
This style needs surfaces that refract, shimmer, veil, reflect, diffuse, or bloom.

### Core materials
- crystal
- mirror
- satin
- silk
- chiffon
- metallic fabric
- reflective water
- fog
- glass
- lacquer
- polished stone
- velvet
- pearl surfaces
- chrome accents used sparingly
- translucent veils

### Material functions

#### Refractive materials
Crystals, glass, prisms create rainbow scatter and sacred geometry light behavior.

#### Reflective materials
Mirrors, still water, polished metal create doubled identity, depth, and metaphysical space.

#### Diffusive materials
Fog, chiffon, haze, clouds soften form and carry light.

#### Glam materials
Satin, jewelry, sequins, metallic textiles deliver ceremonial beauty language.

### Prompt language
- mirrored crystal chamber
- reflective black water
- satin ceremonial robes
- translucent veils in pink haze
- polished silver jewelry catching bloom light
- opaline glass and refractive crystal textures

### Common mistake
Using too many hard industrial materials.
Concrete, tactical leather, brushed steel, and matte black plastics can easily drag the style toward sci-fi noir or generic futurism.

---

## 7. Media DNA

### Definition
The finished image should feel like it has passed through time, format, or reproduction.

### Why it matters
This is what turns a merely "fantasy-glam" image into a recovered artifact.

### Core media traits
- VHS softness
- CRT scanline pattern
- chroma bleed
- tape noise
- mild tracking instability
- faded print
- offset softness
- slight color drift
- airbrush softness
- film grain mixed with analog signal texture
- bloom clipping
- generational loss feeling

### Media principle
The image should feel slightly handled, transferred, screened, printed, copied, or remembered.

### Prompt language
- light VHS degradation
- soft CRT texture
- analog tape softness
- faded paperback print finish
- slight chroma drift
- dreamy generational copy loss
- retro media wear
- damaged-luxury vision

### Common mistake
Overdoing glitches.
This style wants analog softness and memory wear, not aggressive digital corruption or harsh datamosh aesthetics.

---

## 8. Emotional DNA

### Definition
The emotional field of the style is sincere, visionary, romantic, and aspirational.

### Why it matters
Tone determines whether the style feels magical or fake.

### Core emotional states
- awakening
- enchantment
- sacred glamour
- longing
- spiritual destiny
- visionary romance
- cosmic solitude
- divine self-fashioning
- healing fantasy
- ecstatic calm

### Emotional paradoxes
The best images often balance:
- intimacy and spectacle
- sincerity and theatricality
- softness and grandeur
- nostalgia and prophecy
- feminine tenderness and mythic authority

### Prompt language
- transcendent and devotional
- dreamy sacred longing
- romantic cosmic initiation
- visionary serenity
- mystical glamour and wonder
- luxurious spiritual revelation

### Common mistake
Defaulting to irony, parody, sarcasm, or "camp only."
Camp can exist here, but the image still needs faith in its own dream.

---

## How the DNA strands interact

No single strand can carry the style alone.

### If you only have Symbolic DNA
You get generic mystical illustration.

### If you only have Glamour DNA
You get fashion fantasy or editorial surrealism.

### If you only have Optical + Color DNA
You get pretty vaporous AI imagery with no identity.

### If you only have Media DNA
You get retro texture without soul.

### If you combine all of them
You get New Age Glam Fantasy.

The style is strongest when:
- the symbolism gives purpose
- the glamour gives seduction
- the optics give dream-state
- the color gives aura
- the materials give light behavior
- the media layer gives memory
- the emotion gives sincerity

## DNA priority order for prompting

When constructing prompts, this is a useful hierarchy:

### Tier 1: Identity anchors
- symbolic DNA
- glamour DNA
- optical DNA

### Tier 2: Recognition amplifiers
- color DNA
- temporal DNA
- media DNA

### Tier 3: Texture enrichers
- material DNA
- emotional DNA wording
- compositional specifics

If a prompt is too thin, strengthen Tier 1 first.

---

## DNA test

A prompt or image is probably on-model if you can answer yes to most of these:

- [ ] Does it feel tied to a late-70s to mid-80s metaphysical-glam visual world?
- [ ] Is there clear symbolic or cosmic intent?
- [ ] Is beauty/grooming/fashion part of the image logic?
- [ ] Are the optics soft, glowing, and atmospheric?
- [ ] Is the color palette prismatic, pastel-neon, opalescent, or auroral?
- [ ] Do materials support reflection, refraction, diffusion, or shimmer?
- [ ] Is there at least some analog memory texture?
- [ ] Does the emotional tone feel sincere, visionary, or devotional?

---

## Compressed DNA formula

A quick shorthand for the style is:

**symbol + glamour + glow + prism + artifact + transcendence**

Expanded:
**metaphysical symbolism + ceremonial beauty + soft optical radiance + opalescent pastel-neon color + analog media wear + sincere cosmic yearning**

---

## DNA lock statement

If you need one paragraph to keep the whole style stable, use this:

New Age Glam Fantasy is defined by late-70s to mid-80s metaphysical symbolism presented through ceremonial glamour, softened by diffusion-heavy optics, illuminated with prismatic pastel-neon color, grounded in reflective and refractive materials, and finished with analog memory artifacts that make the image feel like a recovered vision of transcendence rather than a clean modern rendering.
